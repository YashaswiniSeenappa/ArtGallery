FOLK STYLE

Folk painting is a vibrant and diverse artistic tradition rooted in the culture and heritage of various communities around the world. It encompasses a wide range of styles, techniques, and themes, often reflecting the daily life, beliefs, and customs of the people. Folk painters typically employ simple, accessible materials such as wood, cloth, or ceramics and use bold, vivid colors to create visually striking compositions.

OIL PAINTING

Oil painting is a traditional and revered artistic technique that has been employed by artists for centuries. It involves the use of pigments mixed with oil, typically linseed oil, as a binder. The slow-drying nature of oil paints allows artists to work with intricate details and achieve rich, luminous colors.

PAINTING

Painting is a multifaceted art form that has captivated humanity for millennia. It involves the application of pigments or colors onto a surface, typically through brushes, fingers, or other tools, to create visual representations of ideas, emotions, or observations.

MODERN

Modern painting, often associated with the developments in art from the late 19th century to the mid-20th century, represents a departure from traditional artistic conventions. It embraces innovation, experimentation, and a break from realistic representation. Modern painters like Pablo Picasso, Wassily Kandinsky, and Salvador Dalí challenged norms with abstract forms, surrealism, and bold color palettes.
